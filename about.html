Behind the lens

Hi, I’m Marco — a researcher currently based at Yale University, where my work focuses on the genetics of behaviours and complex diseases, with a special emphasis on psychiatric disorders. My academic journey began with a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Physics at the University of Pisa. This analytical foundation eventually led me to the University of Fribourg in Switzerland, where I stepped into the fascinating world of bioinformatics, ecology, and evolutionary biology. If you're curious about my scientific work, feel free to explore my research on Google Scholar.

Beyond science, I pursue several passions — particularly sports, nature, and photography. Fencing was my primary sport, practiced competitively for many years since I was a child, and skiing is a passion that has always been with me. Among various sports, I also practiced sleddog racing, taking part in a couple of international competitions, and I have also enjoyed windsurfing and snowboarding as hobbies. These activities allowed me to deepen my connection with nature, the very same bond that I develop today through photography.

My love for photography started early, with a simple Olympia compact camera I used as a child. Over the years, my curiosity and admiration for DSLR photography grew, and in 2021, I finally made the leap and began shooting with one myself. That decision was more than technical. I took that step in memory of a dear friend who had a passion for portrait photography with her DSLR. This journey, and much of my work, is dedicated to her.
